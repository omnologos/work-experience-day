Architecture overview

Alexa-enabled devices run a small local component for wake-word detection. When triggered, audio is sent to Amazon's cloud where speech-to-text and NLU services determine intent. The cloud then either responds directly or invokes a Skill, service, or smart-home API.

Speech recognition & NLU

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR): converts spoken words into text. Natural Language Understanding (NLU): maps text to intents and extracts parameters (slots) so Alexa knows what action to take.

Skills & integrations

Developers build Skills that provide conversational flows. Skills run either in the cloud or via Lambda functions and can access APIs, databases, and third-party services to fulfill requests.

Smart home control

Alexa translates voice commands into standard smart-home directives (on/off, set value). Many devices integrate through cloud-to-cloud APIs or a local hub.

Privacy considerations

Audio is transmitted only after wake-word detection. You can review and delete your voice history in the Alexa app, turn on microphone mute, and control what Skills have permission to access.
