What is Alexa?

Alexa is Amazon's cloud-based voice assistant. It listens for a wake word (for example "Alexa") and responds to spoken requests by answering questions, controlling smart devices, playing media, and running voice apps called Skills.

How Alexa works

  1. Wake word detection: A small local model on the device listens for the wake word and wakes the device when it hears the trigger.
  2. Capture: After waking, the device records a short audio clip of your request.
  3. Transmit: The audio is securely sent to Amazon's cloud for processing.
  4. Natural Language Understanding (NLU): Cloud services convert speech to text, determine intent, and map the request to an action.
  5. Action & response: The cloud returns a response (spoken reply, music playback, or a command to control a smart device).
  6. Device executes: The device speaks the response or performs the requested action (e.g., turns on a light).

Common uses

  • Hands-free information (weather, news, timers, answers)
  • Smart home control (lights, locks, thermostats)
  • Music, podcasts, and media playback
  • Alarms, reminders, and calendar management
  • Third-party Skills for shopping, games, and utilities

Privacy & controls

You can review and delete your voice recordings, mute the microphone on many devices, and control what Skills have access to. Alexa processes requests in the cloud to provide accurate responses; voice recordings and account data are subject to Amazon's privacy controls.

Alexa Developer
  • Alexa Skills are voice apps that extend Alexa's abilities.
  • Wake word detection runs locally; other processing happens in the cloud.
  • Many manufacturers build Alexa into smart speakers, watches, and appliances.
  • Routines let you chain multiple actions to a single command.
  • You can use the Alexa app to customize settings and linked services.
